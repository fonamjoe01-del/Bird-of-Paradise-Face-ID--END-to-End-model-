Bird-of-Paradise

The National Emblem of Papua New Guinea

Introduction

The Bird-of-Paradise is the national emblem of Papua New Guinea (PNG). The species featured on the country's national emblem is the Raggiana Bird-of-Paradise (Paradisaea raggiana). It is admired around the world for its brilliant orange-red feathers, golden-yellow head, emerald-green throat, and elegant courtship displays. The bird is one of Papua New Guinea's most treasured native species and is an important symbol of the country's identity and natural beauty.

National Importance

The Bird-of-Paradise represents freedom, unity, peace, beauty, pride, strength, cultural diversity, and the rich biodiversity of Papua New Guinea. It appears on the National Coat of Arms together with the Kundu drum and ceremonial spear, symbolizing the traditions and heritage of the people. The bird also appears on the national flag, government logos, official documents, and many cultural celebrations.

Scientific Classification

Scientific Name Paradisaea raggiana
Kingdom Animalia
Phylum Chordata
Class Aves
Family Paradisaeidae
Habitat Tropical Rainforests of Papua New Guinea
Average Length 34 cm
Diet Fruits, berries, insects, seeds and small invertebrates

Main Characteristics

  • Bright orange-red ornamental feathers.
  • Golden-yellow head.
  • Emerald-green throat.
  • Long decorative tail plumes.
  • Spectacular mating dances.
  • Excellent climber and flyer.
  • Found mainly in tropical forests.

Interesting Facts

  • More than 40 species of Birds-of-Paradise exist worldwide.
  • Papua New Guinea is home to most Bird-of-Paradise species.
  • Only the male has the brightly coloured ornamental feathers.
  • Males perform elaborate dances to attract females.
  • The Bird-of-Paradise has been a national symbol of Papua New Guinea since independence in 1975.
